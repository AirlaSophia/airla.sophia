Limerence or Real Love

On the difference between obsession and love — and what the energy actually shows.

April 27, 2026
12 min read
· · ·

In all my years doing intuitive and psychic readings, one pattern breaks my heart more than any other.

Someone sits down across from me — or connects with me online — eyes bright, energy electric, absolutely certain. They have finally found the one. They describe the connection in words that sound like poetry. The way they felt the moment they met. The signs they have been collecting like evidence. The certainty that the universe itself orchestrated this meeting.

And then I look at the energy.

And what I see is not love.

What I see is limerence. And I have to find a way to gently, compassionately tell them that what they are feeling — as real and as overwhelming and as all-consuming as it is — may not be what they think it is.

This is one of the most common and most painful things I witness in my work. And I am writing this because I am tired of watching people get hurt. Tired of seeing beautiful, worthy, loving human beings pour themselves into connections that exist primarily in their own minds and hearts — while the other person remains emotionally unavailable, inconsistent, or simply unaware of the depth of feeling directed at them.

You deserve to know the difference. And a reading can help you find it.

What Is Limerence?

Limerence is a term coined by psychologist Dorothy Tennov in the 1970s to describe an involuntary state of intense romantic obsession. It is characterized by intrusive and persistent thinking about another person, an overwhelming need for their reciprocation, and the interpretation of their every word and action as deeply significant.

The critical word in that definition is involuntary. You do not choose limerence. It arrives like a visitation — sudden, overwhelming, and completely convincing.

And here is what makes it so dangerous: limerence does not feel like obsession from the inside. It feels like recognition. It feels like fate. It feels like the most alive you have ever been in your entire life.

This is not a character flaw. This is not weakness. This is neurochemistry — and it is also, as I will explain, something much deeper than that.

What Is Actually Happening in Your Brain

When limerence activates, your brain floods with dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin in patterns that are virtually identical to early stage romantic love. The crucial difference is this: in healthy early love, these neurochemicals gradually stabilize as the relationship deepens and becomes more secure. In limerence, they do not stabilize. They fluctuate wildly — spiking every time the other person gives you attention and crashing every time they withdraw it.

This fluctuation is the engine of the obsession. Every text back is a hit of dopamine. Every period of silence is withdrawal. Over time your brain becomes addicted — not to the person exactly, but to the neurochemical cycle that person triggers in you.

But from the inside, it does not feel like addiction. It feels like passion. It feels like proof that this connection is rare and real and worth fighting for.

The Feeling of Destiny

One of the most universally reported experiences of limerence is an overwhelming sense of recognition — a feeling of I have always known you, or you are the one I have been searching for.

Your nervous system is not saying this person is safe and loving and good for you. It is saying this person matches the blueprint of love that I built in childhood. And those are two profoundly different things.

This is where my work as an intuitive reader becomes so important. Because I can see the difference between a genuine soul connection and a psychological wound wearing the costume of destiny.

The Difference Between Limerence and Real Love

Limerence focuses on how they make you feel. Real love focuses on who they actually are.

Limerence thrives on uncertainty and longing. Real love deepens through consistency and safety.

Limerence intensifies with distance. Real love grows with closeness.

Limerence is rooted in your wounds. Real love is rooted in your wholeness.

The Spiritual Truth Underneath Limerence

Limerence is not a mistake. It is not a malfunction. It is a messenger.

The person who triggered your limerence was not sent to destroy you. They were sent to show you something. They are a mirror — reflecting back the places in you that are still longing for a love you did not receive.

The intensity of the feeling is not evidence that this specific person is your destiny. It is evidence of the depth of what you are longing for. And that longing — that hunger — is sacred.

Are You in Limerence Right Now?

Do you think about this person constantly — even when you are trying not to? Does their response or lack of response determine how your entire day feels? Does their unavailability make you want them more rather than less?

If you answered yes to these questions, I want you to know something important. What you are feeling is real. The intensity is real. The longing is real. And you deserve support in understanding it — not judgment, not dismissal.

You deserve to know the difference between limerence and love. And you deserve to experience the latter — fully, truly, and with someone who is actually available to receive it.
